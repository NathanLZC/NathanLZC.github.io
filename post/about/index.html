🏠 About this website

Welcome to my digital space dedicated to computational mechanics. The insights and content shared here are largely influenced by my academic journey, especially the notes and reflections from my master's program at Centrale Nantes. As I delve deeper into the subject, I'll be continuously updating and enhancing the content of this blog. Whether you're a fellow student, a professional in the field, or simply curious, I invite you to explore, share, and engage in meaningful discussions about the topics presented. Your feedback and perspective are invaluable!

👨‍💻 Who am I

I am currently a first-year PhD student at Institut Jean le Rond d'Alembert, where I am investigating Rough Heterogeneous Contacts: From Micro to Macro. My research is focused on developing and applying FFT-based homogenization methods to analyze the contact behavior of rough heterogeneous interfaces. I have a strong passion for multiscale modeling and its potential to unravel complex material interactions.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I completed a Master's degree in Computational Mechanics at Centrale Nantes. During my Master's, I had the opportunity to work at Institut Jean le Rond d'Alembert on a master thesis titled "Fluage et surfaces rugueuses - évolution du contact entre matériaux viscoélastiques" (Creep and Rough Surfaces – Evolution of the Contact Between Viscoelastic Materials).
